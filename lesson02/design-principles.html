Design Principles Document

  • Design Principle: Visual Hierarchy
  • Company Name: Apple
  • Wbsite:Apple

Apple's website effectively employs visual hierarchy by using varying font sizes and weights to guide users' attention. The primary headline, "iPhone 16 Pro," is prominently displayed, followed by a concise subheadline, "Hello, Apple Intelligence." This structured typography ensures that users first notice the most critical information, facilitating a clear and intuitive navigation experience.

  • Design Principle: PARC: Contrast
  • Company Name: Microsoft
  • Wbsite:Microsoft

Microsoft effectively uses contrast by placing bright-colored call-to-action buttons against neutral or muted backgrounds. This enhances visibility and user engagement. For example, on their homepage, a bold "Shop Microsoft 365" button contrasts sharply with surrounding white space and lighter elements, drawing attention to the most critical actions.

  • Design Principle: Hick's Law
  • Company Name: Amazon
  • Wbsite:Amazon

Amazon applies Hick's Law by organizing its mobile interface into simple, categorized menus and personalized suggestions like "Buy It Again." By presenting users with a manageable number of choices and recommendations, Amazon reduces decision-making time, ensuring a seamless shopping experience.
